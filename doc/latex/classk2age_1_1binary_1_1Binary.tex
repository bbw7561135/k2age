\-Convolve two model mass tracks to get the k2-\/age relation. 

\-The convolution of the two mass tracks takes the weighted average of the individual $k_{2,\, i}$ values. \-This is presented in \-Equation (5) of \-Feiden \& \-Dotter (2013). $\overline{k_{2}} = \frac{c_{2,\,1}k_{2,\,1} + c_{2,\,2}k_{2,\,2}}{c_{2,\,1} + c_{2,\,2}}$.

\-Compute $c_{2, i}$ coefficients. 

\-These coefficients weight the individual k2 values to derive a weighted average of the two binary components. \-They are computed according to \-Equation (2) in \-Feiden \& \-Dotter (2013), $c_{2,\, i} = \left[\left(\frac{\Omega_i}{\Omega_K}\right)^2 \left(1 + \frac{m_{3-i}}{m_i}\right) f(e) + \frac{15 m_{3-i}}{m_i} g(e) \right]\left(\frac{R_i}{A}\right)^5$.
